Life story

Emmanuelle Charpentier was born 11th December 1968, Juvisy-sur-Orge, France. Charpentier grew up in Paris and completed her undergraduate studies at Pierre and Marie Curie University in biochemistry in 1992. Charpentier achieved her PhD in microbiology at Pasteur Institute in 1995.

Key works include:

  • 1996-2002-Researched in America.
  • 2006- Established a research group at the University of Vienna.

CRISPR

In 2020 Charpentier was awarded a Nobel prize in chemistry for her work on CRISPR.

Key highlights of CRISPR

  • CRISPR can be used to alter specific genes within a cell.
  • Gene editing is completed through the usage of the CRISPR Cas9 protein and the target RNA. The RNA acts as a guide to ensure that the target DNA strand is edited.
  • Usage of CRISPR allows for both animal and plants to be edited with specific genes added, removed, turned off or on. The posibilities of CRISPR are numerous.
